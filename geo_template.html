Reading notes

  • This is the real world map — contrast it with the semantic (MDS) map, where position itself encodes similarity. Here position is fixed by geography and similarity is carried only by the lines.
  • Most links are short and regional (US cities to each other, the Gulf states, the Indian metros, the Chinese tier-1 cities), which is why the network reads as a set of national/regional constellations.
  • The interesting links are the long ones that survive a high cutoff — e.g. the Gulf to South Asia, or intra-Anglosphere ties — bridges the news corpus draws across oceans. Push the slider up to isolate them.
  • Hover or tap a city to spotlight just its links and list the cities it connects to at the current cutoff.
